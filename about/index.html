About PhyloGuessr

How it works

Pick which two of three organisms are most closely related — the pair sharing the most recent common ancestor.

Scoring

In standard mode, you score points for a correct answer (reduced by 50% if you used a hint).

In "closest pair" mode (6 species), you score based on how close your chosen pair's common ancestor is compared to all other possible pairs. Closeness is measured by taxonomic rank — a pair sharing a genus is closer than one sharing a family, which is closer than one sharing an order, and so on. Where the common ancestor has an informal rank (like a "clade" node in NCBI Taxonomy), the score is based on the nearest formally-ranked ancestor above it, with tree depth used as a tiebreaker within the same rank.

A note on NCBI Taxonomy

NCBI Taxonomy is a classification, not a true phylogeny — it draws from multiple sources and may not reflect the latest phylogenetic understanding. See the NCBI Taxonomy Help for details.

FAQ

Where did the idea come from?

I attended a Science on Tap trivia night long ago and they did this type of game live with a powerpoint and it was very entertaining, and I thought it would make a fun web game. The name is obviously indebted to GeoGuessr for making the "-guessr" format iconic.

Can I use this for my own trivia night or classroom?

Absolutely! Feel free to use the website or any of the data on it for trivia, teaching, or anything else.

Why does phylogeny matter?

Phylogeny has applications across medicine, agriculture, conservation, forensics, and more. See our Why it matters page for examples and a guide to model organisms.

Is the name inspired by GeoGuessr?

Obviously yes — we are indebted to GeoGuessr for the naming inspiration. They made the "-guessr" format iconic and we happily borrowed it for the phylogenetics world.

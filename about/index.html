About PhyloGuessr

How it works

Pick which two of three organisms are most closely related — the pair sharing the most recent common ancestor.

Data sources

  • Phylogeny from the Open Tree of Life
  • Species names from NCBI Taxonomy
  • Images from Wikipedia

Why Open Tree of Life?

NCBI Taxonomy is a classification, not a phylogeny — it often can't resolve which two of three species are closest (a "polytomy"). The Open Tree synthesizes published phylogenetic studies for better resolution. For example, NCBI leaves primates, colugos, and rodents unresolved within Euarchontoglires, while the Open Tree correctly groups colugos with primates.
